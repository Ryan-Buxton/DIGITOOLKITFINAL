Plight of Indian Farmers

Impact on Farmers

Indian farmer suicides have been on the rise

As many people talk about climate change or sustainability, a largely forgotten group is on the front line of the climate crisis. Farmers in India have been struggling with a plethora of issues for decades and between climate change, the rise of fast fashion, and government policies. Their suicide rates portray a grim picture of their plight- with every drought or drop in rainfall the suicide rates rise. Inversely when they finally do receive the much-needed rain it usually comes in the form of harsh monsoons which can flood and wash away their fields and destroy their crops.


Farmers in India have taken to the streets of Deli, the capital with their tractors to protest the changes to farmers subsidies that the government has been trying to push through. If these bills passed it would have raised the cost of fertilizers and pesticides that are already astronomically expensive for the farmers. With droughts worsening the farmers need every break they can get. Farmer suicide has also increased since a debt forgiveness program, which provides pensions for widows and the children of farmers who die, along with debt forgiveness of up to around 2000 USD. So often times when farmers incur debt equal to the amount able to be forgiven in the case of their death, they kill themselves, so their families are forgiven of their debt and are provided for. Most often they hang themselves or drink the pesticides they went into debt trying to purchase. It is often said by their advocates that having a bottle of pesticides in the house is as dangerous as having a loaded gun.
